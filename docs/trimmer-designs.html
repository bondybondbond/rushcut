Design comparison notes

Aspect Design A (Pantry grid) Design B (Clip list)
Clip navigation 2-col thumbnail grid — visual, scannable Scrollable list — compact, shows "In film" status
Preview size Large — fills centre panel Slightly larger — no pantry competing for space
Trim bar Solid colour (dark/bright regions), minimal Frame-strip texture under handles (implies real frames)
Film assembly Drag to bottom timeline — intuitive "Add to film" button — explicit, beginner-friendly
Best for Visual scanners with many clips Methodical users; clearer status tracking

Key open question for both designs

Is the assembly model "add clips explicitly" (user picks what goes in) or "all clips in, user removes" (current model)? Both designs above use the explicit-add model based on your wireframe. Confirm before building.

Trim bar note

Design B shows a faint frame-strip texture. This is the sprite-sheet approach from the deferred 14e-filmstrip plan — it would need real FFmpeg frame extraction per clip. For the first build, Design A's solid-colour trim track is simpler and still clear.
